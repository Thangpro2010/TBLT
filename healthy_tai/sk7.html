30-day healthy living plan of a fitness coach



The four main points of Coach Joe Wicks' mental and physical fitness plan include eating, exercising, setting goals, and getting good sleep. Joe Wicks is a renowned fitness coach (coach) on The Body Coach channel. His "PE with Joe" program also reached millions of followers on Youtube. Recently, he shared his practice experience as well as a 30-day health care plan called "30 Day Kick Start" to have good fitness for the last days of the year.


1.Reasonable diet, creating comfort


According to Joe, diet should create comfort, should not overdo it, help you enjoy a variety of food sources. "I want you to eat delicious and delicious food, but the key to my plan is consistency: Focus on homemade dishes with reasonable serving sizes, instead of processed foods, drinks that are gas, fast food and alcohol, "Joe said.Besides, minimize the consumption of foods containing starch and sugar. Examples include corn, potatoes, cassava, rice, confectionery and even fruit high in sugar. Replace with protein (protein) and fat in foods such as meat, fish, eggs, milk ... However, you do not need to completely remove carbohydrates from the menu. It is important to enjoy the right carbs after workouts to recharge and strengthen your muscles. Take some time to exercise before meals. For example, if you like a plate of pasta for dinner, schedule an afternoon or early evening workout. If you like breakfast porridge, wake up and exercise in the morning.


2.Exercise and rest in a balanced manner
Joe thinks that there should be a reasonable exercise regimen and appropriate rest. "I exercise five days a week and rest for two days," says Joe. For your body to recover from a workout is important, so the rest days are just as important as the days of exercise for overall health.

3. Set realistic goals
Another key to Joe's 30-day plan success is goal setting. Goals don't have to be big, and they don't have to be based on body weight or weight. If your goals are unrealistic and your expectations are too high, you will be less likely to achieve them. Don't feel discouraged by not achieving something either, as this may lead to you wanting to give up completely. Sometimes the goals are very simple. For example, a possible short-term goal is: "I'm not going to drink a carbonated drink or alcohol today, I'll just drink water" or "I'll go to bed an hour earlier each night this week." . All these small wins really add up and make a big difference in a month or a year.

4. The importance of sleep


After giving birth, Joe shared, he really felt the negative effects of lack of sleep. During those early weeks, Joe found it difficult to practice on a regular basis. "I tried to do something, even if I only walked 20 minutes to clear my mind, but still felt difficult sometimes," Joe recalls. Up to 90% of depressed people have poor sleep, which makes the body tired. It's important to take care of your sleep as well as take care of your body. Pay attention to your space, see if it is peaceful, relaxing and orderly or not? You can use other supportive measures like listening to meditations, turning off your mobile devices and relaxing with music or reading a book before bed.
