People with excess cholesterol still need fat



People with excess cholesterol, should not abstain from fat but should choose healthy fats because they contribute to checking excess cholesterol, protecting liver. The information was shared by Associate Professor, Doctor, Doctor Le Bach Mai, Former Head of National Institute of Nutrition at the launching ceremony "Action month to repel excess cholesterol in the body" by the Ministry of Health. officials in Hanoi. According to Associate Professor, Doctor and Doctor Le Bach Mai, it is not reasonable for those with excess cholesterol to completely abstain from the fat group. Everyone needs to eat fat every day, so know how to use and choose healthy fats, avoid harmful fats, not "choose from" fat. Many families only eat food, which is not good either.




Fat is very essential, required in the daily diet, is an indispensable component to make up cells, especially nerve cells and many hormones in the body. They also provide energy, are solvents help dissolve vitamins, help absorb vitamins into the body. The deficiency of fat in the daily diet affects many parts, especially the nervous organs. In the adult's diet, fat should be about 20-25%. The percentage of animal fat should not exceed 60% of the total dietary fat intake. The group of healthy fats (unsaturated fats) can be mentioned as Omega 3 - 6 - 9, Gamma - Oryzanol and Phytosterol. These are essential fatty acids, antioxidants that help reduce the absorption of cholesterol from foods. But these fatty acids can not be synthesized by the body, but must be obtained from foods containing fat. Cholesterol is actually fat, essential for the body's activities. Cholesterol mainly has 2 main types, good cholesterol (HDL) and bad cholesterol (LDL) with 2 sources produced by the liver itself synthesized (endogenous) and from food (exogenous). Endogenous cholesterol often creates enough cholesterol the body needs, while cholesterol from dietary sources easily causes excess cholesterol. If the fat is controlled, excess cholesterol can be controlled.
