Moderna applies for urgent approval of the Covid-19 vaccine



The Moderna pharmacy said it had filed a request for US and European regulatory authorities to allow the emergency use of the Covid-19 vaccine. On November 30, the company filed an application for the license and the necessary databases with the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The advisory committee will meet to discuss the request on December 17, the agency said. Thus, Moderna's product could be the second vaccine, after Pfizer, to receive US approval and go to delivery this year. Moderna also plans to apply for registration in Europe. The company said the vaccine was effective consistently across age, race, ethnicity and sex. The shots successfully prevent serious cases of the disease.


"We believe we have a highly effective vaccine. Now the company has data to prove that. We expect to play an important role in turning the tide of this pandemic." said Tal Zaks, Moderna's Medical Director. Moderna's experimental vaccine shot at a US laboratory, July 2020. Photo: AP Of the more than 30,000 volunteers participating in the end-stage trial, 196 were infected with nCoV, including 185 who took placebo and 11 were vaccinated, according to its data, according to its data. Moderna reported 30 serious illness cases, all of which were in the placebo group. Alexander Edwards, associate professor of biomedical technology at the University of Reading UK, said: "As the number of cases increases, this amazingly effective product strengthens community confidence".


"While we are waiting for more details, we expect the vaccine to be approved by December," said Gillies O'Bryan-Tear, president of policy and communications at the British College of Pharmacy. Moderna plans to produce about 20 million doses of the vaccine, ready to ship in the US by the end of 2020, enough to vaccinate 10 million people (two shots each). Moderna's vaccine is 94.5% effective, with an expected price tag of $ 37 per shot, almost twice as expensive as the Pfizer / BioNTech vaccine.
