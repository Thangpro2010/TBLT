Arsenal in the round of 1/16 Europa League



Arsenal beat Molde 3-0 in the fourth match of the Europa League group stage on the evening of November 26, thus soon gaining the right to enter the knockout round.

With 12 points in four games, Arsenal soon completed their group stage goal. Coach Mikel Arteta's team also only needed one more point in the next match with Rapid Wien to ensure that they could keep the top of Group B. Contrary to the volatile form in the Premier League, Arsenal showed a completely different face when playing in Europa League. 12 of their last 13 goals are in the continental arena, the other in the Premier League is Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang's penalty kick against Man Utd.

Aubameyang was not in the squad to Molde, leaving a spearhead for juniors Eddie Nketiah. The 21-year-old player paired with Alexander Lacazette, and Nicolas Pepe was assigned to play in the right midfield. The layout of Coach Arteta helps "Gunners" create many attacking options, in the context that Molde is forced to seek a victory to maintain the opportunity to continue.

The home side entered the game not badly and almost opened the scoring in the 15th minute when Sheriff Sinyan finished out in front of the open goal. On the other side of the goal, Arsenal strikers also missed a few delicious situations after a quick counterattack. Pepe, who was red carded in the match against Leeds, played aggressively as if to atone.

At the beginning of the second half, Arsenal's expensive contract had a technical ball from the right wing before cutting the ball of his left foot to hit the bar. Pepe did not have to regret long because soon after, the French striker received the ball in the penalty area and then tore Molde with another set of his left foot. This is Pepe's third goal after four Europa League appearances this season.
