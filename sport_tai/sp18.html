Milan was held back without Ibrahimovic



AC Milan drew 1-1 as Lille's guest in the fourth match of Group H Europa League on November 26.


The desire to avenge the 0-3 defeat at the San Siro earlier this month was clearly shown by Milan right after the opening whistle. The visitors came up to attack with many arrangements in two sides. However, the lack of Zlatan Ibrahimovic in the middle position made Milan's crosses ineffective. Ante Rebic has technical moves with his feet, but the 1.85 meter high striker is not strong enough in every dogfight.


That made Milan stuck in the first half, in the game Lille accepted to play under. The French team took a deep step back in the defensive formation and patiently waited for the opportunity to counterattack. The speed of the players above helped Lille create some clear dining opportunities. Goalkeeper Gianluigi Donnarumma, wearing the captain's armband for the first time, made at least two excellent saves at the end of the first half to keep Milan from conceding first.


At the beginning of the second half, the goal came to Milan when Sandro Tonali put a beautiful slit to Rebic to face the goalkeeper, before passing Castillejo to easily cushion the ball into the empty net. Milan soon had the opportunity to double the gap, but Hakan Calhanoglu's kick from the narrow corner brought the ball to lick the post out.
