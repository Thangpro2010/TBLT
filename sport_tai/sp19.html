Disabled swimmer with limbs



According to Curin, the limb disabled swimmer is the youngest member of the French national team to participate in the Paralympic Games.

At the age of 6, Curin was diagnosed with meningococcal encephalitis, a rare disease that leads to blood clotting and tissue death. In order to save the patient's life, the doctor was forced to surgically remove the extremities of Curin.

Growing up, a physical defect could not prevent Curin from fulfilling her dreams. With extraordinary efforts, Curin became a swimming grandmaster, the youngest athlete representing France to participate in the 2016 Paralympic Games in Rio, Brazil.

Walking proficiently on prosthetic legs, Curin recalls playing for the first time in Brazil: "This is an extraordinary thing."

Before getting sick, Curin was obsessed with water. "I used to hate water a lot. I didn't usually do swimming classes at school because I was too scared," he admitted.

This fear changed when he met disabled athlete Philippe Croizon at the age of 7. Philippe encouraged Curin to learn to swim and explore his full potential.
