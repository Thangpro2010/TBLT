President Trump is about to hit the 300 mark of golf when in office



Over the weekend, President Trump spent two consecutive days golfing at his Virginia-owned golf course. According to Golf News Net, this is the 296 and 297 Trump's visit to a golf course since he became the 45th President of the United States on January 20, 2017.


Trump reportedly moved to the golf course of the same name just before 10:00 a.m. on November 14 and repeated that the next day. On the same day, Trump also sent various messages, saying Biden first won the election via a Twitter post but later said he did not admit to anything and continued to accuse election fraud.


A full week ago, when the representative of Biden Democrat was declared victory by the major media, based on the results of the vote count of each state, Mr. Trump was also on the golf course. Even when "lost", Trump continued to his golf course in Virginia.


Trump has only three more such trips left to hit the 300 mark on a golf course during his tenure. In his first year as President, Trump played golf 91 times. The number in the second year is 76 and the third year is 91. It all took place at Trump's golf course, except for a joint battle with Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe and professional star Hideki Matsuyama (November / 2017), the same time remained with Mr. Abe in May 2019.
